1. Genre Definition: Meta on top of Simplicity

Arcade-Idle is a genre that combines hyper-casual level simple controls and rules with Idle's meta loop of numerical economy and territory expansion. This achieves both the low entry barrier of hyper-casual and the high LTV (Lifetime Value) of Idle.

Core Hybrid Points:

  • From HC: Simple controls (joystick), low failure stress, fast onboarding
  • From Idle: Economy-based progression, upgrades/unlocks, automation desire, snowball growth

4. System Design: Simplify and Expose Depth

The key to successful Arcade Idle planning is the harmony between "Simplify" and "Expose Depth".

Design Pillar Detailed Design Points
Controls/UX Focused on 1-stick (move) + automatic interaction (auto-pickup). UX is extremely simplified to focus on "one task at a time."
Economy An inflationary structure where unlock costs increase exponentially while earnings also grow. Creating choke points to induce payment/ads through non-linear sinks.
Progression Packaging the map by Zone is important to make users believe "there is a long-term goal" early on.

5. Content Extension: Vertical vs Horizontal Strategy

When extending content, you must balance user learning burden and freshness.

Vertical (Addition)

Increasing variations within the same step (e.g., adding pizza menu while selling burgers). Low risk and useful for filling volume quickly.

Horizontal (Addition)

Adding new process steps (e.g., inserting 'packaging' step after cooking). High perceived depth but used only at major inflection points as it may disrupt flow.

6. Monetization: The Art of Speed-up

Because Arcade Idle users have a strong desire for progression, all BM is focused on "Acceleration."

  • Ads (IAA): "Watch to go faster". Proposing rewarded ads as a solution to user pain points.
  • In-app Purchases (IAP): "Permanent Boost". Selling permanent versions of ad-based rewards (Ad-free, Golden Tool, etc.).
  • Subscription: Exclusive content + passive bonuses. Sometimes accounts for over 30% of IAP revenue.

7. Summary: Action Items for Success

  • Early 3 minutes: Did you show locked zones and power-up results in advance to build anticipation?
  • Economic Balance: Do revenue inflation and spending choke points work together to stimulate the desire to watch ads?
  • Content Pacing: Does it maintain stability with vertical variations and refresh interest with horizontal variations in new zones?
