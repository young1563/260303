Top Mobile Games of 2025: Casual, Hypercasual & Midcore

Published: Early 2026 | Source: MAF.ad Analysis

1. Market Overview

The mobile gaming landscape in 2025 continued to be dominated by established giants while showing clear shifts in monetization strategies. Block Blast! secured the spot for the most downloaded game for the second consecutive year with over 368 million downloads, a significant +65.7% increase YoY.

On the revenue side, Honor of Kings by Tencent took the crown with nearly $1.7 billion in IAP revenue, proving that high download numbers are not always a prerequisite for massive financial success.

2. Hypercasual Trends

Downloads: The Reign of .io Games

The list of most downloaded hypercasual games highlights a preference for simple, fast-paced, and multiplayer experiences. 4 out of the top 10 titles were .io games, characterized by straightforward onboarding and shared arena gameplay.

  • #1 Pizza Ready! (166M Downloads)
  • #2 Hole.io (121M Downloads)
  • #3 Snake Clash! (104M Downloads)

Revenue: The Shift to Hybrid-Casual

While simple games dominate downloads, the revenue leaders tell a different story. Top-grossing "hypercasual" titles like Color Block Jam ($107M) and Screwdom ($75M) lean heavily into puzzle mechanics.

This trend signifies the rise of Hybrid-Casualâ€”combining simple core loops with deeper meta-layers and complex gaming elements to boost IAP monetization. Mob Control stands out as a unique case, appearing in the top 10 for both downloads (#7) and revenue (#5), successfully bridging mass appeal with strong monetization.

3. Top Grossing Publishers

The publisher market remains highly concentrated. Tencent leads with over $5.7 billion in revenue, nearly triple that of the second-place publisher, Century Games. While Chinese publishers (Tencent, Century Games, NetEase) dominate the top ranks, Western companies like Scopely, Roblox Corporation, and Dream Games continue to maintain strong market positions.

4. Conclusion

2025 reinforced the divergence between viral hits and monetization powerhouses. While purely hypercasual games still capture massive audiences, the sustainability of revenue is increasingly found in hybrid models that offer deeper engagement layers.
