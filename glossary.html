Game Glossary

Defining key industry terms and insights.

FTUE

Core Concept

FTUE (First-Time User Experience) refers to the initial stage when a user first encounters and uses a product or game. It includes the first impression, tutorials, registration processes, and the early journey to finding the core "fun" or value.

FTUE is a critical factor in determining user retention and long-term engagement. The goal is to provide easy and clear navigation, deliver core value quickly, and offer a positive first experience.

Key Elements & Importance of FTUE

  • First Impression & Experience: The comprehensive experience within the first few minutes, including loading screens, UI, and tutorials.
  • Impact on Retention: A well-designed FTUE prevents early churn and encourages continuous play by inducing positive emotions.
  • Delivering Core Value: Quickly letting users experience the game's core value gives them the motivation to move to the next stage.

Strategies for Improvement

  • Easy & Fast Start: Minimize complex logins and long tutorials; get users into gameplay as quickly as possible.
  • Intuitive Learning: Use tutorials to teach rules easily without overwhelming the user with too much information at once.
  • Rewards & Achievement: Provide clear goals and rewards in early stages to create a sense of accomplishment.

Analysis & Measurement

  • Data-Driven Analysis: Analyze churn rates at each tutorial stage and first-session data to find bottlenecks.
  • Retention Metrics: Evaluate FTUE effectiveness through Day 1 Retention.

In summary, FTUE is not just a tutorial; it is the first step in making users understand and love your product, and should be a top priority in development and design.
